A rhythm, not a nag

Every twenty minutes of screen time, your eye muscles deserve twenty seconds of distance. Offscreen turns that into a rhythm you never have to think about.

Focus

The menu bar quietly counts down your work interval. Pick a preset — Balanced, Deep Focus, the classic 20-20-20 — or tune every number yourself.

Heads-up

A minute before each break, a small panel slides in without stealing your keyboard. Start early, or snooze +1, +5, +15 minutes — within your snooze budget.

Break

Every display softens into a countdown and a gentle prompt. Twenty seconds later you're back — eyes rested, shoulders down, posture reset.

Know your screen, keep your privacy

Offscreen tracks the things worth knowing — how long you've been on screen, which apps eat your day, whether you're actually taking breaks — and distills it into one daily Screen Score.

Nothing leaves your Mac. There's no account, no telemetry, and — unusually — no permission prompts at all: no Accessibility, no Screen Recording, no camera or microphone access. Ever.

Questions, answered

Why do I need screen breaks at all?

Staring at a fixed distance keeps the focusing muscles in your eyes contracted for hours, which drives digital eye strain — dryness, blur, headaches. Optometrists suggest the 20-20-20 habit: every 20 minutes, look at something about 20 feet away for 20 seconds. Offscreen exists to make that habit automatic.

Will it interrupt my meetings or games?

No. Smart Pause holds breaks while your camera or mic is in use, media is playing, your screen is shared, or a fullscreen app is up — and you can add your own "never interrupt" apps. Due breaks fire as soon as you're free.

What permissions does it ask for?

None. Every detector is built on permission-free system APIs, so macOS never shows a privacy prompt for Offscreen. It can't see your screen contents or read your keys — by design and by architecture.

Where does my data live?

In ~/Library/Application Support/Offscreen/ — a human-readable settings JSON and a SQLite stats file you can open yourself. Nothing is sent anywhere.

Is this Lookaway?

No — Offscreen is an independent, personal open-source project inspired by Lookaway, which deserves the credit for pioneering this thoughtful approach to screen breaks. If you want a polished commercial app with support and an iPhone companion, go buy it. Offscreen is not affiliated with or endorsed by its makers.
