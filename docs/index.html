Smart Presets

Balanced (20 min / 20 s), Deep Focus (45 min / 30 s), 20-20-20, or a fully custom rhythm — with a longer break every few cycles.

Skip Difficulty

From Casual (skip anytime) to Hardcore (no skipping), with an optional auto-lock so long breaks actually get you to step away.

Cursor Countdown

A subtle countdown pill follows your cursor in the final seconds before a break, so it never catches you mid-thought.

Planned Breaks

Schedule named breaks — lunch, a walk — at fixed times of day, using the same gentle heads-up flow.

Office Hours

Set active hours and days so Offscreen only tracks and reminds you during your actual workday.

Screen Score

A simple 0–100 metric for how well you're resting your eyes today, with a 7-day history — all stored locally in SQLite.

Deep Customization

Tune break lengths, overlay style, and rotating messages — plus an optional ambient sound that plays when a break ends.

Power Shortcuts

Global hotkeys to take a break now (⌥⇧B) or toggle pause (⌥⇧P) from any app, plus launch at login.

Multi-Display Support

Break overlays cover every display — even over full-screen apps — across dual monitors and ultra-wides.

Smart Pause. It knows when you're busy.

Offscreen doesn't blindly count time. It reads permission-free system signals to hold a break until you're actually free.

  • Pauses when your Mac is locked, asleep, or idle
  • Holds during meetings (camera or mic in use), screen sharing, and full-screen apps
  • Keeps going while you play music — Spotify or Apple Music isn't a meeting

Frequently Asked Questions

Does it use the camera or track my eyes?

No. Offscreen never accesses your camera, microphone, or screen contents. It only reads permission-free system signals — like whether the camera is in use (a meeting) or how long you've been idle — so macOS never even shows a privacy prompt.

Can I use it alongside a Pomodoro workflow?

Yes. Run it as your main timer with the Deep Focus preset, or in a lighter 20-20-20 eye-rest rhythm alongside another tool.

Is there a mobile version?

No — Offscreen is macOS-only, focused on computer-related eye strain. For your phone, the built-in Screen Time features are a good fit.

What is the Screen Score?

A weighted daily metric based on your break consistency and adherence to eye-rest guidelines. Above 80 is excellent. It's computed and stored entirely on your Mac.

How do I install and uninstall it?

Install with brew install --cask dayaki/tap/offscreen (or download from Releases). Remove it with brew uninstall --cask offscreen — no leftover daemons or config files.

Is this Lookaway?

No. Offscreen is an independent, open-source project inspired by Lookaway, which pioneered this thoughtful approach to screen breaks. Offscreen isn't affiliated with or endorsed by its makers.
